# Example Criteria for Map Submission

A high-quality map submission should have:

1. **Data Representation**
   - All 5 stations plotted and labeled by name
   - All geographic points plotted (colored by category)
   - Geographic relationships are clear and accurate

2. **Cartographic Elements**
   - North arrow: Simple arrow or compass rose clearly indicating north
   - Scale bar: Labeled with distances (km or miles), positioned clearly
   - Coordinate grid: Latitude/longitude lines or UTM grid, labeled at edges
   - Map boundary: Clear extent of the map area

3. **Visual Design**
   - Station locations distinct from point categories (different marker shape/size)
   - Colors for point categories are distinct and accessible
   - Legend showing:
     - Station names or markers
     - Categories of points with colors
   - Professional appearance, not cluttered

4. **Labeling**
   - All 5 station names labeled on the map (readable, not overlapping if possible)
   - Legend clearly showing point categories
   - Title: "Manitoba: Station and Point Distribution"

5. **Geographic Accuracy**
   - Points appear in correct relative positions
   - Bounding box appropriately covers all data
   - Coordinates/projection clearly labeled or implied

6. **Technical Quality**
   - High resolution (≥300 dpi for print, or 96 dpi at minimum 350 pixels wide)
   - Saved as PNG with good compression
   - Map dimensions appropriate for a journal figure (~150mm × 150mm for square, or ~180mm × 120mm for landscape)
